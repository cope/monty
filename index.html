Monty Hall problem simulation

This is a simple simulation of the Monty Hall problem.

Pick a number of iterations and click Simulate.
The larger the number, the longer it will take for the simulation to complete.

In each iteration, the simulation will:

  1. Randomly put a car behind one door
  2. Randomly pick one door
  3. Randomly open a non-picked, non-car door
  4. Randomly decide whether to switch or not
  5. Note Win (W) or Lose (L)

At the end of the simulation, the sum of Ws and Ls will be equal to the selected number of iterations.
The percentages of Ws and Ls will be displayed.

Delay (ms): Anything below 10ms will be ignored.
Delay affects total simulation time.
